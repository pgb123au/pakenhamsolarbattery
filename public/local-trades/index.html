In-network trades

The Pakenham trades we coordinate with on every job.

Pakenham Roof Restoration — the concrete-tile-side of the job.

We won't bolt a solar array onto Pakenham concrete tiles older than ~25 years without a roof-condition photo or recent re-pointing — the 2003-2008 estates are the ones most likely to have cracked ridge caps that the array brackets will exacerbate. If the roof needs flexible-pointing or a re-coat before the panels go on, roof restoration in Pakenham hold the concrete-tile end of that work — we hand off, they hand back.

Pakenham Electricians — the emergency electrical-side of the job.

Battery commissioning needs a dedicated RCD sub-circuit and, on the 2026 inverter rules, a CT-clamp export-limiter and an emergency-stop accessible from outside the meter box. Most Pakenham switchboards built before ~2010 don't have RCDs on power circuits and need a switchboard upgrade first. Pakenham Electricians hold the emergency electrical end of that work — we hand off, they hand back.

Councils, regulators & standards

The Cardinia Shire and the SE-Melbourne corridor authority map.

We point homeowners to these resources constantly — the regulator register and the Australian Standard are the two documents every Pakenham solar PV array quote should reference.

  • Clean Energy Council (CEC) — the accreditation body for solar PV designers + installers. Without CEC-accredited install you cannot claim STC rebate. www.cleanenergycouncil.org.au
  • AS/NZS 5033 — PV array installation — the Australian Standard for PV array installation, governing array DC-side wiring, isolators, and earthing. store.standards.org.au
  • AS/NZS 4777 — Grid connection of energy systems via inverters — the standard for AC-side inverter grid-connection — battery export-limit rules live here. store.standards.org.au
  • Solar Victoria (Solar Homes Program) — the Victorian rebate + interest-free loan scheme for solar + battery. Eligibility is means-tested. www.solar.vic.gov.au
  • AusNet Services connection portal — the Pakenham-region distribution network. Every new inverter > 5kW needs a pre-approval from AusNet. www.ausnetservices.com.au
  • Cardinia Shire Council — for planning permits in heritage overlay zones — only relevant if the panels are visible from the street in a heritage precinct. www.cardinia.vic.gov.au
Frequently asked

How Pakenham trades sequence around solar PV array.

Does my Pakenham roof need work before solar?

If the roof is concrete tile and older than ~25 years, yes — almost always. The 2003-2008 Pakenham estates have brittle ridge mortar that cracks when the array brackets shift in summer heat. We require a roof-condition photo or recent re-pointing certificate before we'll bolt panels on. Colorbond and recent-tile (post-2015) roofs usually need no prep.

Will my switchboard need an upgrade for a battery?

If your switchboard has ceramic fuses or no RCDs on the power circuits — yes. The 2026 AS/NZS 4777 rules require a dedicated RCD sub-circuit for the inverter, a CT-clamp export limiter, and an emergency-stop outside the meter box. Switchboards built post-2015 almost always have RCDs and need no upgrade; pre-2010 ones almost always do.

How does the Solar Homes rebate work in 2026?

Solar Victoria pays the rebate to the Accredited Provider, netted off the install price. The 2026 rebate is means-tested (household income cap) and applies to both solar PV and battery systems separately. We quote the rebate-net price after eligibility check — bring your most recent ATO Notice of Assessment to the quote.

Why do I need AusNet pre-approval?

AusNet Services is the electricity distribution network in the Pakenham region. Any new inverter that can export > 5kW into the grid needs a connection pre-approval before we can energise the system. AusNet sometimes imposes a 5kW export limit on the inverter, which we configure via the CT clamp during commissioning.
