## Version 2 — Operator voice, technical credibility

Five things every Pakenham solar & battery quote should answer before you sign:

1️⃣ **CEC accreditation.** Only a Clean Energy Council accredited installer can claim STCs and the federal battery rebate. No accreditation = no rebate = you overpay. Ask for the accreditation number.

2️⃣ **Which rebates are deducted.** Three stack in Victoria: solar STCs on the panels, the federal Cheaper Home Batteries Program (~$252/kWh in 2026) on the battery, and Solar Victoria eligibility. A good quote shows each as an upfront discount, not a "claim it later".

3️⃣ **United Energy approval.** Pakenham, Officer and the Cardinia Shire are in the United Energy network. Every grid-connected system needs DNSP pre-approval and AS/NZS 4777 export design before it's energised. Some areas have export limits — which is exactly why a battery makes sense here.

4️⃣ **System sizing logic.** The corridor's large north-facing estate roofs make 10–13kW easy. The mistake is undersizing for an EV or battery you'll add later. Sizing should be to your real bills and load curve, not a package.

5️⃣ **Standards.** AS/NZS 5033 (PV array), AS/NZS 4777 (grid connection), AS/NZS 5139 (battery safety), AS/NZS 3000 (Wiring Rules), with a Certificate of Electrical Safety. A real installer rattles these off.

Without these five, you're buying on price alone — and usually missing thousands in rebates.
